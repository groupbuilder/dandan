Biography of Current Officers and BOD

 
Sue Chauis an environmental planner, focusing on California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) and National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) projects related to water, recycled water, and wastewater. She has prepared a variety of environmental impact assessments for local, state, and federal agencies in California. She currently works for RMC Water and Environment. Sue received her degree in Environmental Science from UC Berkeley. Sue is serving on the board of directors for CAEPA.

Jim Leu received a Ph.D. in Environmental Engineering from Pennsylvania State University. He has more than 20 years of experience in soil and groundwater remediation including environmental assessment, remedial investigation, feasibility study, pilot studies, remedial design, remedial action implementation, and remedial process optimization. He has the US and international consulting experiences including Australia, Hong Kong, Thailand, Taiwan, and China. Jim is a licensed Professional Engineer in California, Oregon, Pennsylvania, and Taiwan. He is also a certified LEED® AP. He has more than 15 papers published in the US and international journals and conferences. Currently, Jim is a project manager of Parsons. Jim is serving on the board of directors for CAEPA.

Xinggang Tong obtained B.S. in Chemical Engineering from Hunan University, China in 1982. He came to U.S. in the same year and studied in the Environmental Engineering and Science Program at Stanford University and obtained M.S. in 1984 and Ph.D. in 1992. He has since worked full time in private environmental consulting firms in the San Francisco Bay Area: Wahler Associates from 1989 to 1994, URS Corporation (formerly Woodward-Clyde) from 1994 to 2005, and OTG EnviroEngineering Solutions, Inc. from 2005 to current. His main expertise is in soil and groundwater remediation, industrial wastewater treatment, and landfill gas and leachate controls. Xinggang is serving as the President of CAEPA for Year 2014-2015.
Back to top
 
Kevin Jim received his bachelor's degree in civil & environmental engineering from the University of California at Berkeley, and his master's degree in environmental engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. He currently works for Black & Veatch in Walnut Creek. From 2004 to 2007, Kevin worked at Black & Veatch's Hong Kong office. He has been primarily involved in the planning and design of water and wastewater treatment plants. A member of CAEPA since 2003, Kevin currently serves in its program committee. He has assisted in the planning of numerous CAEPA dinner meetings and annual meetings. Kevin is also a violist and performs regularly throughout the Bay Area.
Back to top
 
Ray Yep has almost 40 years of experience in the water, wastewater, and environmental field. He started his career when the Clean Water Grants program was starting and he has worked on planning and design at the EBMUD, San Jose, Palto Alto, Santa Rosa, SF Oceanside and Southeast, and other wastewater treatment plants. Most recently, Ray was the Water Utility Operations Division Manager at the Santa Clara Valley Water District. In that role, he was responsible for delivering drinking water to the greater San Jose/Santa Clara area. Ray was also the Asia Pacific Regional Managing Director for CH2M HILL for 10 years and has lived in Taipei, Tokyo, and Singapore.

Ray has a BS/MS in civil/environmental engineering and is a registered PE. While he is currently semi-retired, he previously worked for Kennedy/Jenks, CH2M HILL, Parsons, and the Santa Clara Valley Water District. Ray is currently the Chair of Berkeley's Public Works Commission.

Sanjay Reddy has an MS in Civil/Chemical Engineering and an MBA in Finance. He has over 20 years of experience and specializes in the planning, design and construction of water and recycled water facilities. He is a project manager with Black & Veatch in the Walnut Creek Office and is actively managing projects in the greater Bay Area. His most recent project includes the design and construction of a 10 MGD microfiltration, reverse osmosis, ultraviolet disinfection Advanced Recycled Water Treatment Facilities for the Santa Clara Valley Water District/City of San Jose. Sanjay is the Chair of the American Water Works Association Residuals Committee and WateReuse Association 2010 Technical Program Planning Committee. When Sanjay is away from work or work related activities, he is probably hiking trails or climbing mountains. He is an avid backpacker and through hiked the 220 mile John Muir Trail in the summer of 2009.

Shin-Roei Lee works at the San Francisco Bay Regional Water Quality Control Board. She has worked in most major Board programs, including wastewater and storm water permitting, watershed protection, and soil and groundwater cleanup.

She is currently the Chief of the Watershed Management Division and has been in this position since 2003. She is in charge of implementing the statewide industrial storm water permit, construction storm water permit, Caltrans permit, and Phase I and Phase II municipal storm water permits. In addition, she is responsible for stream and wetland protection, recycled water, and onsite wastewater treatment programs in the San Francisco Bay region.

She has a bachelor's degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering and a master's degree in Environmental Engineering. She is a Registered Civil Engineer and Qualified Storm Water Pollution Prevention Plan Developer and Practitioner in California.

Diane Wang earned her bachelor's degree in biological and environmental engineering from Cornell University, and her master's degree in environmental engineering from Stanford University. She currently works for WRECO in Walnut Creek, providing engineering services including bridge hydrologic and hydraulic studies, roadway drainage design, technical analyses and modeling. Her previous job as an engineer at EA Engineering, Science, and Technology, Inc., as well as her previous Engineering Co-op position at Wetland Studies and Solutions, Inc., had provided her with invaluable experience in environmental site assessment, investigation and remediation, wetland delineation, stream restoration, and stormwater management. In her free time, she enjoys reading, dancing, and outdoor adventures. Diane is now serving as the Vice President of CAEPA for Year 2014-2015.

Ivy Tao is currently pursuing her Ph.D in Environmental Engineering in University of California, Berkeley. Her research focuses on the effects of pollutant emissions from the transportation sector on regional air quality. A native of China, she has studied in England and Philadelphia before moving to California. She is also passionate about clean energy development and building sustainable communities. Ivy became involved with CAEPA in 2012 and loves the organization for its rich international heritage and engineers with bold visions.
